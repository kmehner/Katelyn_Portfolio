A little bit about me

I'm born and raised in Thousand Oaks, California. At home, my support system consists of my parents, my sister, and a few dogs I got in trouble for bringing home. I grew up skiing, hiking, and sailing with my dad (I'm just along for the ride). In highschool, I participated in Y&G and the IB program, and played water polo. In fall of 2018, I moved to Colorado to attend CU Boulder and fell in love with it here. Freshman year, I joined Alpha Phi Sorority, went skiing, and attempted to figure out what I wanted to do with my life. The summer of 2019, I was lucky enough to study abroad in Rosario, Argentina. I lived with a host family and attended a local school to study the history and culture of the country. I learned so much during my time there, and worked on my spanish skills along the way. When Covid hit during my sophomore year in 2020, school went online and I decided to stay in Colorado (mostly to avoid quarantine with a little too much family time). Almost two years later, I've graduated with a Bachelor's Degree in Economics and minors in Business and Spanish. For the past few years, I've worked at Boulder Country Club. After grauting, I started working full-time there. I've rotated between Pool manager, front-desk associate, and a server. Most of all, I still don't have a plan for what I want to do with my life, but I'm finally starting to get a little closer
